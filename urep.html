About UREP

The Undergraduate Research Education Program (UREP) at Wood Hudson Cancer Research Laboratory provides exceptional opportunities for undergraduate students to engage in meaningful cancer research while developing essential skills for careers in science, medicine, and healthcare.

Our program combines rigorous scientific training with mentorship from experienced researchers, giving students the tools they need to succeed in graduate school, medical school, and professional careers in cancer research.

Program Structure

The UREP program is designed to provide comprehensive research training through a structured yet flexible approach that accommodates student schedules and academic commitments.

1

Orientation & Training

Introduction to laboratory safety, research protocols, and cancer biology fundamentals.

  • Laboratory safety certification
  • Research methodology training
  • Introduction to cancer biology
  • Equipment and technique training
2

Project Assignment

Match with research project based on interests and career goals.

  • Meet with potential mentors
  • Review available projects
  • Define research objectives
  • Establish timeline and milestones
3

Active Research

Conduct research under mentor guidance with increasing independence.

  • Execute experimental protocols
  • Collect and analyze data
  • Attend research meetings
  • Develop technical skills
4

Presentation & Publication

Present findings and contribute to scientific publications.

  • Prepare research presentations
  • Contribute to manuscripts
  • Present at conferences
  • Career planning guidance

Program Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Currently enrolled undergraduate student
  • Minimum 3.0 GPA
  • Completed basic science coursework (Biology, Chemistry)
  • Strong academic standing
  • Faculty recommendation

Time Commitment

  • Minimum 10 hours per week during academic year
  • Full-time summer research opportunities available
  • Flexible scheduling to accommodate classes
  • Minimum one-semester commitment
  • Attendance at weekly research meetings

Personal Qualities

  • Strong interest in cancer research
  • Commitment to scientific excellence
  • Good communication skills
  • Ability to work independently and in teams
  • Attention to detail and safety protocols
